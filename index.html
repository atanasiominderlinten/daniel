Focused on three interconnected fields — harpsichord and early keyboards, organ, and musicological research — my work bridges historical performance and contemporary creation. At the harpsichord, my practice centers on continuo accompaniment and ensemble conducting from the keyboard. On the organ, symphonic repertoire and orchestral transcriptions hold a special place in my practice. In musicology, my research focuses on editorial work and the principles of musical notation. Passionate about multidisciplinary projects, my activity spans mainly Italy and Spain.

super flumina: songs of life and nature

visual concert for percussion, piano & organ

This multisensory project for organ, piano, and percussion — with integrated lighting design — explores water as a symbol of life, drawing on Psalm 137, Gregorian chant, contemporary repertoire, and free improvisation.
